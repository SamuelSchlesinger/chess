# Transposition algebra: research recommendation

The strongest result of this branch is a finite-presentation theorem with a
concrete chess measurement:

> The pinned opening graph's 798 redundant history vertices are generated by
> exactly 205 independent typed path relations. A rooted arborescence plus one
> equation per non-tree edge is complete for all root-originating paths, and
> the cycle-space rank proves that no smaller equation set can work while the
> graph's move arrows are retained.

This is structurally stronger than observing that openings form a graph. There
are 570 non-singleton endpoint fibers, but only 193 vertices are fresh merge
points; 377 fibers inherit multiplicity from an earlier merge. The 193 merge
nodes have indegrees `181 x 2` and `12 x 3`, giving total excess indegree 205.

The algebra also reveals why an ordinary trace monoid is the wrong starting
point. Adjacent single plies never commute because every ply flips the
side-to-move token. The first useful local relation is an alternating
three-ply braid `abc ~= cba`. Even that is incomplete: the existing Lean
witnesses prove both same-length route substitution and unequal-length detour
cancellation.

## What is already known

Trace monoids, causal equivalence, partial-order reduction, convergent
rewriting, and category presentations are established theories
[aalbersberg88][aalbersberg88] [flanagan05][flanagan05]
[clerc15][clerc15]. Chess engines have long recognized repeated positions with
transposition tables [zobrist90][zobrist90], and published opening-book work
already uses position DAGs [fishburn18][fishburn18]. None of those general
ideas is a novelty claim.

The plausible contribution is their chess-specific synthesis: the alternation
obstruction, an exact minimum relation basis for a named opening graph, a
measured braid/substitution/detour decomposition, Lean-certified witnesses,
and a controlled test of whether relation-aware training improves human
move-order transfer. A scoped search found no prior source with that
combination; this is not an exhaustive priority claim.

## Exact pilot result

| Quantity | Count |
|---|---:|
| Distinct histories | 8,646 |
| Exact repetition nodes | 7,848 |
| Directed projected edges | 8,052 |
| History excess | 798 |
| Non-singleton history fibers | 570 |
| Fresh merge nodes | 193 |
| Minimum chord relations | 205 |
| Chords preserving length and UCI multiset | 201 |
| Same-length substitution chords | 3 |
| Length-changing detour chords | 1 |
| Chords that are one alternating braid | 84 |

Of the 205 shortest-path basis relations, 86 have at most three divergent
plies per side and 138 have at most five. Thus the minimum-cardinality basis is
promising but not automatically human-readable.

## Ranked research shortlist

| Rank | Program | Novelty case | Player value | Fast falsifier |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Relation-aware personal repertoire and trainer** | Strong if the minimum basis plus route exceptions improves transfer | High: removes duplicate cards and teaches when move orders differ | A 30-position, four-week matched trial in the existing trainer |
| 2 | **Alternating-braid schema discovery** | Plausibly new chess-specific causal presentation | Medium-high if a few plan schemas explain most of 201 compatible chords | Legal braid BFS on only the 205 basis pairs; kill if fewer than 70% have a derivation of at most four braids |
| 3 | **Certified partial-order reduction for opening analysis** | Technically credible application of POR to an alternating game | Medium: less duplicate engine work, but mostly backend value | Compare explored states and preserved adversarial deviations on a small repertoire |
| 4 | **Directed homology or higher-dimensional invariants** | Mathematically interesting | Low unless an invariant predicts study burden or strategic flexibility | Correlate invariants with human recall or repertoire coverage before formalizing |

The recommendation is to pursue ranks 1 and 2 together. Rank 1 supplies the
player-facing success criterion; rank 2 supplies explanations and formal
content. Do not pursue pure homology merely because the cycle rank is easy to
compute.

## Immediate theorem and experiment sequence

1. In Lean, prove the no-adjacent-commutation theorem for colored legal plies.
2. Define sound alternating braids and prove their length/multiset invariants.
3. Formalize the generic arborescence normalization and 205-rank lower-bound
   interface; keep the empirical graph certificate separate.
4. Search legal, not merely catalog-visible, braid intermediates for the 205
   basis relations.
5. Optimize the canonical arborescence for a personal repertoire and human
   explanation length rather than UCI shortlex.
6. Upgrade `~/projects/games/chess/src/bin/chess-trainer/book.rs` from raw
   prefix matching to an exact position index plus a separate route/deviation
   index.
7. Run the matched transfer trial before expanding the exercise inventory.

## Known limitations

- The name catalog is sparse and unweighted; its counts are not game
  frequencies or evidence of move strength.
- “Same length and UCI multiset” is only a necessary condition for causal
  permutation equivalence.
- The current braid closure uses only catalog-visible intermediate histories
  and is therefore an under-approximation.
- The 205 basis minimizes relation count, not explanation length or review
  burden.
- Human benefit remains an experimental hypothesis. Exact algebra cannot by
  itself supply positional plans, opponent likelihoods, or a path to a rating
  target.
