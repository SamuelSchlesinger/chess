# Transposition algebra: research recommendation

The strongest result of this branch is a rooted-path normalization theorem with
a concrete chess measurement:

> The pinned opening graph has 798 duplicate prefix occurrences. Endpoint
> equality on all root-originating paths of its fixed, edge-retaining projected
> graph is generated by exactly 205 concrete typed equations. A rooted
> arborescence plus one equation per non-tree edge is complete, and the
> cycle-space rank proves that no smaller set of concrete endpoint-sound
> equations can work under that declared counting model.

This is not a lower bound on hash-table deduplication, parameterized schemas, or
a presentation of every hom-set in the full path category. The complete 205
equations and their executable checks are published in the branch certificate.

This is structurally stronger than observing that openings form a graph. There
are 570 non-singleton endpoint fibers, but only 193 vertices are fresh merge
points; 377 fibers inherit multiplicity from an earlier merge. The 193 merge
nodes have indegrees `181 x 2` and `12 x 3`, giving total excess indegree 205.

The algebra also reveals why an ordinary trace monoid is the wrong starting
point. Adjacent single plies never commute because every ply flips the
side-to-move token. The first local reordering relation considered here is an
alternating three-ply braid `abc ~= cba`. Even that is incomplete: existing
Lean witnesses prove independent examples of same-length route substitution
and unequal-length detour cancellation, although they do not certify all four
exceptional records in the selected basis.

## What is already known

Trace monoids, causal equivalence, partial-order reduction, convergent
rewriting, and category presentations are established theories
[aalbersberg88][aalbersberg88] [flanagan05][flanagan05]
[clerc15][clerc15]. Chess engines have long recognized repeated positions with
transposition tables [zobrist90][zobrist90], and published opening-book work
already uses position DAGs [fishburn18][fishburn18]. None of those general
ideas is a novelty claim.

Position-keyed transposition-aware training is also established practitioner
prior art [cpt14][cpt14] [gambitlab26][gambitlab26]. The plausible contribution
is narrower: the alternation obstruction, an exact minimum concrete rooted
relation basis and certificate for a named opening graph, a syntactic
stratification followed by legal-derivation search, Lean-checked mechanism
examples, and a controlled test of explicit relation/deviation prompts against
a strong position-keyed baseline. A scoped search found no prior source with
that combination; this is not an exhaustive or reproducible priority claim.

| Quantity | Count |
|---|---:|
| Distinct histories | 8,646 |
| Exact repetition nodes | 7,848 |
| Directed projected edges | 8,052 |

| White decision nodes, history -> repetition key | 3,091 -> 2,741 |
| Black decision nodes, history -> repetition key | 3,102 -> 2,736 |
| Non-singleton history fibers | 570 |
| Fresh merge nodes | 193 |
| Minimum concrete rooted chord relations | 205 |
| Signature-compatible chords, derivation unclassified | 201 |
| Same-length, different-multiset chords | 3 |
| Unequal-length chords | 1 |
| Chords that are one alternating braid | 84 |
| Distinct local catalog-visible braid relations | 87 |
| Contextual catalog-visible braid applications | 296 |

Of the 205 shortest-path basis relations, 86 have at most three divergent plies
per side and 138 have at most five. The disjoint curriculum buckets are 84
direct-and-very-short braids, two other very-short relations, 52 further
four-to-five-ply relations, and 67 longer relations. Thus the
minimum-cardinality basis is promising but not automatically human-readable,
and its `201/3/1` syntactic split can change with the arborescence.

## Ranked research shortlist

| Rank | Program | Novelty case | Player value | Next discriminating test |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Relation-aware personal repertoire and trainer** | Plausible only for explicit relation/deviation prompts beyond established position-keyed trainers | Potentially high if measured review time falls or unseen-route transfer improves without more mistakes | First a 30-position N-of-1 feasibility check; then a preregistered component-randomized effect study |
| 2 | **Alternating-braid schema discovery** | Plausibly new chess-specific rooted causal presentation | Medium-high if a few plan schemas explain most of 201 signature-compatible chords | Legal braid BFS on only the 205 basis pairs; treat 70%/four braids as a preregistered pilot rule and report threshold sensitivity |
| 3 | **Relation-guided opening-graph exploration** | Unestablished until an alternating-game preservation theorem exists | Possibly less duplicate engine work, but route pruning can lose adversarial options | Define the observation and prove reachability/language/value preservation before comparing explored states |
| 4 | **Directed homology or higher-dimensional invariants** | Mathematically interesting | Low unless an invariant predicts study burden or strategic flexibility | Correlate invariants with human recall or repertoire coverage before formalizing |

The recommendation is to pursue ranks 1 and 2 together. Rank 1 supplies the
player-facing success criterion; rank 2 supplies explanations and formal
content. Do not pursue pure homology merely because the cycle rank is easy to
compute.

## Immediate theorem and experiment sequence

1. In Lean, prove the no-adjacent-commutation theorem for colored legal plies.
2. Define sound alternating braids and prove their length/multiset invariants.
3. Formalize the generic rooted arborescence normalization and conditional
   205-rank lower-bound interface; check the emitted empirical certificate
   against it.
4. Search legal, not merely catalog-visible, braid intermediates for the 205
   basis relations.
5. Optimize the canonical arborescence for a personal repertoire and human
   explanation length rather than UCI shortlex.
6. Upgrade `engine/src/bin/chess-trainer/book.rs` from raw
   prefix matching to an exact position index plus a separate route/deviation
   index.
7. Run the N-of-1 feasibility check, then preregister a component-separated
   transfer study before claiming player benefit or expanding the inventory.
8. Use partial-order-reduction terminology only after proving which
   alternating-game observation the exploration preserves.

## Known limitations

- The name catalog is sparse and unweighted; its counts are not game
  frequencies or evidence of move strength.
- “Same length and UCI multiset” is only a necessary condition for causal
  permutation equivalence.
- The `201/3/1` syntactic split belongs to the selected shortest-shortlex
  arborescence; another minimum basis can classify differently.
- The current braid closure uses only catalog-visible intermediate histories
  and is therefore an under-approximation.
- The 205 basis minimizes relation count, not explanation length or review
  burden.
- The 798 reduction is over all prefix occurrences, not player cards. The
  side-specific decision-node reductions are structural opportunities only.
- Human benefit remains an experimental hypothesis. Exact algebra cannot by
  itself supply positional plans, opponent likelihoods, or a path to a rating
  target. Position-keyed training is prior art; only the additional
  relation/deviation intervention is under test.
- “Partial-order reduction” remains an analogy until a theorem proves that
  pruning relation-equivalent routes preserves a declared adversarial
  observation.
