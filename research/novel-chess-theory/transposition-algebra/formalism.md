# From move strings to an alternating path algebra

The right algebraic object is not a monoid of freely reorderable chess plies.
It is a typed path category whose equations come in at least three kinds:
alternating commutations, route substitutions, and removable detours. This
distinction is forced by chess itself, not chosen for mathematical ornament.

## The baseline: paths and endpoint equality

Fix a legal starting position. Let objects be exact FIDE repetition positions
and arrows be legal moves. A legal move history is then a typed path; path
composition is concatenation when endpoints match. Two root-originating paths
are **endpoint-equivalent** when they end at the same repetition position.
FIDE Article 9.2.3 makes this equality sensitive to the player to move, piece
placement, castling rights, and effective en-passant rights [fide23][fide23].

This is naturally a path category followed by an endpoint projection. A
quotient that identifies every pair of parallel paths is a thin reachability
category. Category presentations modulo path equations are standard rewriting
objects [clerc15][clerc15]; the proposed contribution is the chess-specific
choice, measurement, and use of the equations.

There is an important scope boundary. A repetition position is enough to
determine legal continuations, but not every game outcome: the half-move clock
and earlier occurrences affect 50/75-move and repetition draws. The opening
algebra is therefore a legality and opening-preparation quotient, not a claim
that complete FIDE game histories are interchangeable.

## Why the ordinary trace monoid is degenerate

Mazurkiewicz trace theory starts with words over an alphabet and identifies
`uabv` with `ubav` whenever letters `a` and `b` are independent. The quotient
is a free partially commutative monoid, and a trace can be represented as a
partial order of event occurrences [aalbersberg88][aalbersberg88]. This is the
right model for different schedules of genuinely independent events.

At the granularity of one colored chess ply, however, there are no nontrivial
adjacent commuting squares:

> **Alternation obstruction.** Suppose actions remember which color moves.
> If `a` and `b` are both enabled in position `s`, both are owned by
> `s.turn`. After executing `a`, the turn belongs to the opposite color, so
> that same action `b` is not enabled. Hence both `ab` and `ba` cannot be
> legal from `s`.

Equivalently, the side-to-move field is a global scheduler token written by
every ply. A static independence relation on raw plies is empty, so its trace
monoid is just the free monoid. This explains why saying that opening moves
“commute” is intuitively useful but formally imprecise.

State-dependent independence and dynamic partial-order reduction are well
established in verification [flanagan05][flanagan05]. Chess adds an alternating
adversary, so the scheduler cannot simply be discarded.

## The first useful local relation: an alternating braid

The smallest parity-preserving local equation has three plies:

```text
a ; b ; c   ~=   c ; b ; a
```

Here `a` and `c` belong to one player, `b` belongs to the opponent, both words
are legal from the same position, and their endpoints are equal. For example:

```text
1. Nf3 d5 2. g3   ~=   1. g3 d5 2. Nf3
```

Call this an **alternating braid**. It is state-dependent: a relation valid in
one position need not remain valid after a different prefix. Soundness must
therefore include legality of both paths and exact endpoint equality.

An alternating braid preserves path length and the multiset of atomic UCI
plies. Any equivalence generated by such braids has the same invariants. It
captures causal reordering in the sense that the same move occurrences happen
in a different order; modern rewriting work treats permutation equivalence as
a form of causal equivalence [vanoostrom23][vanoostrom23].

## Two mechanisms that braids cannot express

### Route substitution

The existing Lean corpus proves this Caro-Kann equality:

```text
1. e4 c6 2. d4 d5 3. Nc3 dxe4 4. Nxe4 Nd7
1. e4 c6 2. d4 d5 3. Nd2 dxe4 4. Nxe4 Nd7
```

The knight visits different squares and the UCI move multisets differ. The
paths coalesce, but they are not reorderings of the same events. This requires
a genuine path substitution equation.

### Detour cancellation

The Lean corpus also proves that a Catalan/Bogo route with
`...Bb4+ Bd2 ...Be7` reaches, two plies later, a position reached directly by
`...Be7`. The paths have lengths 17 and 15. Any length-preserving trace or
braid theory misses this relation. Algebraically it is a typed detour
cancellation, not commutation.

These examples are checked in
`Chess/Theory/OpeningCorpusExamples.lean`. They give a strict hierarchy:

```text
alternating braid equivalence
        subset of same-length permutation-compatible endpoint equality
        subset of same-length endpoint equality
        subset of all endpoint equality
```

## A finite presentation and normal forms

For a finite rooted directed graph `G = (V,E)` in which every vertex is
reachable, choose a rooted arborescence `T`. Let `tau(v)` be the unique tree
path from the root to `v`. For every non-tree edge `e : u -> v`, add one typed
equation

```text
tau(u) ; e   ~=   tau(v).
```

> **Arborescence normalization theorem.** These chord equations generate
> exactly endpoint equality on all root-originating paths in `G`.

The proof is a one-line path induction with an important typing condition. If
`p = q;e`, first normalize `q` to `tau(u)`. If `e` is the chosen tree edge into
`v`, the result is definitionally `tau(v)`; otherwise apply the chord equation.
Every generator preserves endpoints, proving soundness. Thus any two root
paths to `v` normalize to the same `tau(v)`.

Choose each `tau(v)` to be shortest and then lexicographically least. Orient
every chord equation toward `tau(v)`. The shortlex measure strictly decreases,
so normalization terminates, and the theorem supplies the unique normal form.
This is a specialized convergent presentation; general completion procedures
go back to Knuth and Bendix [knuth70][knuth70], while the termination-plus-local
confluence route is Newman's classical result [newman42][newman42].

## Why the number of chords is also a lower bound

For a connected underlying undirected graph, the binary cycle-space dimension
is

```text
beta_1 = |E| - |V| + 1.
```

The arborescence has `|V|-1` edges, hence exactly `beta_1` chords. Each chord
equation has a boundary equal to its fundamental cycle, and these cycles form
a basis. Conversely, in any presentation that keeps the graph's arrows and
uses endpoint-sound parallel-path equations, the equation boundaries must span
the cycle space: otherwise some fundamental root-path equality remains
underived after linearization over `F_2`. At least `beta_1` equations are
therefore necessary. The chord construction reaches that bound.

This does **not** mean that cycle-space algebra alone proves normalization.
The cycle space forgets arrow direction, move labels, endpoints, path
composition, and legality. An arbitrary linear cycle basis need not lift to
directed parallel chess paths. The arborescence construction does lift, and
the path-induction proof—not the rank calculation—establishes the directed
congruence result.

## Formal theorem candidates

The smallest useful Lean development is:

1. define a colored legal action and prove the alternation obstruction;
2. define `AlternatingBraidAt s a b c` and prove legality, endpoint soundness,
   symmetry, and closure under legal left/right contexts;
3. prove that braid closure preserves length and move multiset;
4. instantiate the existing Caro-Kann and Catalan witnesses as strict
   obstructions to braid-only completeness;
5. formalize the arborescence normalization theorem for a finite rooted typed
   graph, then connect corpus certificates to it;
6. define a shortlex normalizer and prove termination, soundness, and unique
   normal forms.

The algebra becomes chess theory only when its generators are interpreted.
The next question is not whether a 205-equation basis exists—it must—but
whether those equations cluster into a small vocabulary of plans that players
can recognize and reuse.
