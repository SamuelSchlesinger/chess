# A research program for novel, useful chess theory

This corpus asks where exact state modeling, formal proof, engines, game data,
and human experiments can produce chess knowledge that is both accurate and
useful to a serious player. The target is not novelty-by-formalization. A result
must say something about chess that could have been false before the
investigation, and its assurance level must match the evidence behind it.

Three independent investigations were authored, reviewed, and revised:

- [Transposition algebra](transposition-algebra/index.md) studies relations
  between move histories and gives a concrete minimum rooted basis for the
  pinned opening graph.
- [Opening decisions](opening-decisions/index.md) turns route exposure,
  repertoire coverage, commitment, and transposition reuse into falsifiable
  player-facing quantities.
- [Certified chess knowledge](certified-chess-knowledge/index.md) compares
  opening research with proof-producing tactics and tablebase theorem mining.

The original reviews and the revision disposition are preserved in
[reviews](reviews/README.md).

## What survived review

### 1. The opening graph has a small exact rooted relation basis

The pinned opening-name corpus contains 8,646 distinct histories but only 7,848
exact repetition positions. Its fixed edge-retaining projection has 8,052
edges. For all paths starting at the initial position, endpoint equality is
generated by exactly 205 concrete typed equations: choose a rooted
arborescence, then add one equation for every non-tree edge. The cycle-space
rank proves that no smaller set of concrete endpoint-sound equations suffices
under this declared model.

This is the strongest completed structural result. It is a minimum concrete
**rooted path basis**, not a presentation of every hom-set in the full path
category, a lower bound for parameterized rewrite schemas, or evidence that a
player must learn 205 cards. The executable
[basis certificate](transposition-algebra/data/rooted_path_basis.json) records
all equations and endpoints.

The classification also gives a useful chess-specific lesson. Ordinary
adjacent plies cannot commute because every ply changes the side to move. The
first local reordering mechanism is instead an alternating three-ply braid
`abc ~= cba`. In the chosen basis, 84 chords are one such braid; 87 distinct
catalog-visible braid relations have 296 contextual applications. The
remaining signature-compatible relations still need legal derivations rather
than labels inferred from move multisets.

### 2. A transposition shares future knowledge, not route history

The opening-decision pilot measures two different effects:

- White has 3,091 history decision nodes and 2,741 repetition-key decision
  nodes, a differential of 350 (11.32%).
- Black has 3,102 history decision nodes and 2,736 repetition-key decision
  nodes, a differential of 366 (11.80%).
- Among projection-matched histories reaching the same endpoint, the sum of
  curated branch incidences differs in 247 of 270 White-side pairs and 220 of
  265 Black-side pairs. All-legal summed incidence differs in 32 and 98 pairs.

The first result is position-node deduplication, not a measured reduction in
cards, memory, or study time. The second shows route-sensitive structure in a
curated taxonomy, but it is neither unique-move-set inclusion nor proof that a
player can force one route against a state-dependent opponent. Together they
justify a better model: merge endpoint-dependent knowledge at exact positions,
retain complete histories as route and deviation provenance, and measure human
transfer before calling the quotient a cognitive saving.

### 3. Exact semantics can catch practical engine errors

FIDE repetition identity and Polyglot book hashing are not interchangeable.
FIDE ignores a raw en-passant target when no **legal** en-passant capture is
available; the Polyglot convention can still hash the file when only the
adjacent pawn geometry is present.

The corpus now includes a legal history from the initial position in which an
en-passant capture is pseudo-legal but king-illegal. After two knight cycles,
the exact FIDE position occurs three times while the legacy Polyglot key counts
only two. Lean validates the intended identity, Python independently replays
the witness, and Rust regression tests demonstrate the old count and the
repair. The engine now uses structural repetition equality, with a normalized
Zobrist value only as an equality prefilter; its transposition table continues
to use Polyglot hashing.

This is a completed cross-validated semantic diagnostic, not yet a Lean theorem
about that concrete game history and not a novel strategic chess theorem. It
does demonstrate why formal semantics, executable oracles, and engine tests
belong in one repository.

### 4. Theorem mining remains promising, but its novelty bar is narrow

Using tablebases to find counterexamples and repair human endgame rules is
established prior art. A publishable contribution therefore needs the
conjunction of a new exact chess predicate, a canonical or minimal exception
family, proof under fully declared semantics, and evidence that the compact
rule transfers to unseen positions. The revised branch freezes a KPK control
and a KPKP discovery protocol—including domains, label semantics, feature
grammar, train/test separation, complexity budgets, and kill thresholds—before
labels are mined.

Bounded tactical certificates remain valuable shared infrastructure. Their
checker must model legal defenses, claim-now and claim-after-move draws,
automatic draws, checkmate priority, stalemate, and dead positions. A checked
principal variation by itself is neither a forced-mate proof nor new chess
knowledge.

## Recommended program

The practical and research tracks should now advance together:

1. **Build the graph-aware trainer.** Replace raw main-line playback with exact
   position decisions plus separate route/deviation records. Compare explicit
   relation and deviation prompts against a strong position-keyed baseline;
   position-keyed training itself is prior art.
2. **Measure real opening decisions.** On the player's games and a held-out,
   rating- and time-control-matched sample, estimate encounter coverage,
   supported deviation risk, engine regret, study units, and unseen-route
   transfer. Do not prescribe a named repertoire from taxonomy density alone.
3. **Formalize the structural spine.** Prove no adjacent-ply commutation,
   define sound alternating braids, formalize rooted-arborescence normalization,
   and check the emitted 205-equation certificate in Lean.
4. **Run one frozen theorem-mining pilot.** Reproduce the non-novel KPK control,
   then attempt the declared KPKP corridor only if the pipeline yields a short
   exact rule and survives prior-art review.
5. **Keep claims typed by assurance.** Distinguish Lean theorem instances,
   checked certificates, pinned oracles, engine estimates, database estimates,
   and human hypotheses in every exported guide or training item.

## Evidence boundary

| Claim type | Current status |
|---|---|
| Complete FIDE game-state semantics and generic repetition-key equivalence | Lean-checked |
| Legal-history witness and exact FIDE count of three | Cross-validated by a Lean fixture, Python replay, and Rust tests |
| Legacy Polyglot count of two on that history | Cross-validated by Python replay and a Rust regression |
| 205-equation rooted basis and opening-graph aggregates | Reproducible finite computation with a checked artifact |
| Route-sensitive summed branch incidence in the named-opening corpus | Reproducible corpus measurement |
| Lower human study burden after position quotienting | Untested human hypothesis |
| One move order or repertoire is better for this player | Untested empirical judgment |
| A new compact tablebase-derived chess rule | Preregistered research target, not yet a result |

## Reproducible artifacts

- [Transposition classifier](transposition-algebra/data/classify_transpositions.py)
  validates graph counts and drift-checks the complete basis artifact.
- [Opening-decision pilot](opening-decisions/data/pilot.py) measures decision-node
  differentials and summed route branch incidences.
- [Certified-knowledge checks](certified-chess-knowledge/data/check_corpus.py)
  validate the research corpus, while the pinned repetition oracle replays the
  full en-passant history.
- The root `scripts/check_all.sh` checks shared data integrity, builds Lean,
  validates the pinned corpora, tests the Rust engine, and reruns all pilots.

## Status

Authoring, independent review, revision, and executable convergence are
complete for this research checkpoint. The corpus now makes a narrower but
stronger claim than its first draft: it contains one concrete algebraic result,
one corrected structural opening measurement, and one end-to-end semantic
repair. The initial private player import, bounded diagnostic pass, and
persistent six-card review pilot are now complete. Player-specific repertoire
recommendations and claims of training benefit remain deliberately unfrozen
until the declared held-out and human tests pass.
